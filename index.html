Universe Overview

Welcome to the TSM Documentation Hub

TSM is not a single movie. It’s a sprawling network of digital films, spin-offs, interactive games, and community-made lore that grows as new Scratchers join the saga.

Collaborative Storytelling

Production mirrors real-world cinema: creators claim scenes, build them in Scratch, and merge them into full-length movies.

Multimedia Everywhere

The universe jumps between animated films, interactive mini-games, wikis, and in-universe broadcasts to keep the story alive between releases.

Scratch City & Beyond

The saga begins in Scratch City but expands to Mars, the Museum of Natural Scratch History, and the mysterious "Real World".

Join the Canon Crew

Anyone can contribute. The documentation hub keeps track of canon, spin-offs, and community releases so new creators can jump in fast.

Narrative & Lore

The Mythic Backbone of TSM

High-stakes adventures, remix culture, and multiversal threats define the canon.

Dimensional Scratcher Belt

The ultimate MacGuffin: a metaphysical artifact that alters reality. In TSM 1, demon antagonist 666 steals it to dismantle the world.

The Glitch Apocalypse

TSM 2 unleashes the Learning with Pibby-inspired arc. Villain Gimpsty destroys the belt, triggering zombie-like corruption across the universe.

Multiversal Crossovers

Original characters join reimagined icons from Disney and BabyFirst TV, giving the universe its signature syncretic mix.

Signature Locations

Scratch City is the anchor point, but every sequel expands the map with Mars, museums, and portals to the real world.

Glitch Survivors

The apocalypse arc turns heroes into resistance fighters, navigating corrupted zones and protecting remaining safe hubs.

Future Threads

Upcoming releases weave in a portal to reality, foreshadowing how the Scratchers could cross into our world.

Key Characters

Heroes, Legends, and the Glitch Resistance

The cast fuses original heroes with reimagined media icons.

KingDCNT

Founder and lead protector. Wields the Array Display in battle, and his eyes glow red when he’s pushed to the edge.

Googoo

A BabyFirst TV icon recast as a battle-hardened Glitch Slayer with a tactical IQ of 100.

Casey Jr.

The sentient 1940s circus locomotive who uses All Engines Go physics to leap tracks and cross dimensions.

Kaytlin365

Former on-screen hero turned real-world developer. Now the lead builder of TSM’s apps, websites, and interactive experiences.

The Cat Spies

Tactical team formed to navigate the Glitch Apocalypse, gathering intel in unstable, corrupted realities.

Gimpsty

Queen of the Glitches. Her destruction of the belt sparks the apocalypse and raises the stakes of TSM 2.

Production Methodology

How Scratchers Build a Universe

TSM combines amateur tools with professional-style pipelines.

Modular Scene Building

Each movie is partitioned into Scratch Studio projects. Different Scratchers build scenes in parallel, then stitch them into a feature-length release.

Audio Evolution

The franchise started with AI voices but now features human voice actors from the community for richer emotional performances.

Studio-Like Leadership

Producers coordinate timelines, review scene quality, and keep the Scratch Studio organized like a real production board.

Documentation-First

Wiki updates, canon notes, and lore guides stay in sync so the community always knows what’s next.

Community & Governance

A Third Space for Youth Creators

The Scratchers Movie is a third space for youth creators to build, lead, and learn together.

Resilience & Leadership

The franchise weathered major obstacles, including the temporary account ban of founder KingDCNT. Community leaders like tsmiscool stepped in to keep production moving.

Canon Wars

Fans actively debate what counts as canon. GooGoo’s Quest was once declared non-canon, then reinstated by another creator, highlighting the depth of engagement.

Learning & Mentorship

Scratchers teach one another animation, sound design, and leadership, creating a supportive learning network.

Governance Tools

Polls, shared docs, and studio check-ins keep decisions transparent and give the community a voice.
